### CTIS (Cisco Threat Intelligence Summary) for SideCopy APT

#### Overview
SideCopy is an advanced persistent threat (APT) group that has evolved its tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to target government entities, particularly in the Indian subcontinent. The group is known for its use of multiple infection vectors and custom Remote Access Trojans (RATs).

---

#### Key Findings

**Evolution of Tactics:**
- **Initial Infection Vector:** Started with a custom RAT named CetaRAT.
- **Current Infection Vectors:** Utilizes a variety of infection techniques, including LNK files, self-extracting RAR executables (EXEs), and MSI-based installers.
- **Malware Set:** Combines custom and commodity RATs and plugins, indicating a focus on espionage.

**Targeting Tactics:**
- **Themes:** Military, diplomatic, and honeytrap themes.
- **Targeted Entities:** Government entities within the Indian subcontinent.

**Credential Theft and Espionage:**
- **Credential Access:** Focuses on harvesting credentials and sensitive information.
- **Post-Infection Plugins:** Uses a variety of plugins post-infection to maintain access and gather intelligence.

---

#### Technical Details

**Credential Access:**
- **Kits Used:** Custom and commodity RATs, including plugins for credential theft and espionage.

**Persistence:**
- **Techniques:** Uses multiple infection chains to ensure persistence and evade detection.

**Discovery:**
- **Techniques:** Employs various discovery techniques to map the network environment post-infection.

**Lateral Movement:**
- **Techniques:** Utilizes compromised credentials to move laterally within the network.

**Collection:**
- **Techniques:** Focuses on data exfiltration and espionage, collecting sensitive information.

---

#### Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

**Malware Samples:**
- Custom RATs (e.g., CetaRAT, njRAT variants)
- LNK files, self-extracting RAR EXEs, MSI-based installers
