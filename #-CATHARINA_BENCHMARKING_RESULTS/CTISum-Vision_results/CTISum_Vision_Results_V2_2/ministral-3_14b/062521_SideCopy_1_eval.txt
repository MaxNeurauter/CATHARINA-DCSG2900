### CTIS (Cisco Threat Intelligence Summary) for SideCopy APT

#### Overview
SideCopy is an Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group that has evolved its tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) over time. Initially, it used a simple infection vector to deliver a custom Remote Access Trojan (RAT), but it has since diversified its infection chains and malware set.

#### Key Points

**Evolution and Tactics:**
- **Initial Infection Vector:** Custom RAT (CetaRAT).
- **Current Infection Techniques:** Utilizes multiple infection chains, including LNK files, self-extracting RAR EXEs, and MSI-based installers.
- **Malware Set:** Combination of custom and commodity RATs and plugins.
- **Targeting:** Focuses on espionage, targeting military, diplomatic, and honeypot entities, indicating a continued focus on government entities in the Indian subcontinent.

**Credential Theft and Espionage:**
- **Credential Theft:** Uses plugins to steal credentials and sensitive information.
- **Espionage:** Employs a variety of post-infection plugins to conduct espionage activities.

**Credential Access and Theft:**
- **Credential Access:** Uses plugins to harvest credentials from infected systems.
- **Credential Theft:** Targets sensitive information, indicating a focus on espionage.

**Credential Access and Theft via Plugins:**
- **Custom Plugins:** Utilizes custom plugins for credential theft and espionage.
- **Commodity Tools:** Uses commodity RATs and plugins to enhance their capabilities.

**Targeting Themes:**
- **Military, Diplomatic, and Honeypots:** Similar to APT36, SideCopy targets government and military entities.
