### CTIS (Cisco Threat Intelligence Summary) for SideCopy APT

#### Overview
SideCopy is an Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group that has evolved its tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) over time. Initially, SideCopy used a simple infection vector to deliver a custom Remote Access Trojan (RAT), CetaRAT. The group has since diversified its infection chains and malware arsenal, incorporating multiple RATs and plugins.

#### Key Tactics and Techniques

1. **Infection Vectors and Malware:**
   - **Initial Infection:** Utilizes various infection vectors such as LNK files, self-extracting RAR archives (EXEs), and MSI-based installers.
   - **Malware Variants:** Employs a variety of custom and commodity RATs and plugins, including CetaRAT, njRAT, and others.
   - **Credential Theft:** Focuses on credential harvesting and espionage, targeting government entities.

2. **Targeting:**
   - **Primary Targets:** Military, diplomatic, and honeypot targets, indicating a focus on government entities in the Indian subcontinent.
   - **Themes:** Similar to APT36, SideCopy uses multiple themes in their targeting tactics.

3. **Credential Access and Espionage:**
   - **Post-Infection Plugins:** Uses a variety of plugins post-infection, indicating a focus on espionage activities.
