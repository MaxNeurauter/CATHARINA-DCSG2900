### **2. Assessment**
#### **a) Network access: Allow anonymous SID/Name translation (Disabled) – **Secure**
- **Explanation:** Disabling anonymous SID/Name translation prevents attackers from enumerating users/groups via **SAMR (Server Message Block)** without authentication.
- **Security Impact:** **Secure** – This is a recommended setting to prevent unauthorized user/group discovery.

#### **b) Network security: Do not store LAN Manager hash value on next password change (Enabled) – **Secure**
- **Explanation:** This setting ensures that **LM hashes** (which are weak and vulnerable to brute-force attacks) are not stored during password changes. This mitigates risks associated with **pass-the-hash** attacks.
- **Security Impact:** **Secure** – This is a best practice to enforce **NTLMv2** or **Kerberos** authentication only.

#### **c) Network security: Force logoff when logon hours expire (Disabled) – **Misconfigured**
- **Explanation:** Disabling this setting allows users to remain logged in even after their **logon hours** (as defined in AD) expire. This can lead to unauthorized access if an attacker exploits a session after hours.
- **Security Impact:** **Misconfigured** – While not critically vulnerable, this weakens session management and could contribute to **privilege escalation** if combined with other misconfigurations.
