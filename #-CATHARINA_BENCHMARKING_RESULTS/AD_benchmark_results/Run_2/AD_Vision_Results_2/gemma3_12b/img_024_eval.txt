**3. Attack Path:**

*   **Kerberoasting:** This is the primary attack path.  When the "Account is sensitive" attribute is *not* set, the user's Kerberos Ticket Granting Ticket (TGT) is not encrypted with a data encryption key (DEP). This is known as a TGT not being encrypted.  This allows an attacker, who has obtained a hold of the user’s TGT, to decrypt the TGT and extract the user’s account name. This then allows the attacker to request service tickets (STs) for other services on behalf of the user. If those service tickets are not protected by a strong password (often the same as the user’s account password), an attacker can easily compromise those services.

**Technical Impact:** An attacker could impersonate the user and access resources protected by services using those service tickets.  The lack of encryption on the TGT significantly reduces the difficulty of this attack.
