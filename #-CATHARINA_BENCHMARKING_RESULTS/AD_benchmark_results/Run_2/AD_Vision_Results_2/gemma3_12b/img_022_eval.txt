**3. Attack Path & Technical Impact:**

The unchecked "Account is sensitive" flag significantly increases the risk of attacks like **Kerberoasting**.

*   **Kerberoasting Explained:** Kerberoasting is an attack that exploits Kerberos service accounts. When "Account is sensitive" is unchecked, a Kerberos service account (which this user implicitly represents in this context) is not secured against offline password guessing.  An attacker can request a TGT (Ticket Granting Ticket) for the user. The attacker then dumps the password hashes of the Kerberos service accounts from the Key Distribution Center (KDC). They use brute-force or dictionary attacks to crack these hashes and obtain the service account passwords.  Once the attacker has the service account password, they can use it to impersonate that service and gain access to sensitive resources.

*   **Technical Impact:**  Successful Kerberoasting allows an attacker to:
    *   Impersonate the user.
    *   Access resources accessible to the user.
    *   Potentially escalate privileges to domain administrator level if the compromised service account has elevated permissions.
    *   Move laterally within the network.
