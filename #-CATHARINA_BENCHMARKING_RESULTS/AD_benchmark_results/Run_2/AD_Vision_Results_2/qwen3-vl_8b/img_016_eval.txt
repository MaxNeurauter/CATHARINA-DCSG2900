### **Assessment: Misconfigured**  
The **primary issue** is the **10-hour maximum lifetime for service tickets**. This is a known security vulnerability:  
- Service tickets (used for authenticating user accounts to services) are valid for 10 hours by default.  
- **Kerberoasting attacks** exploit this by requesting service tickets for user accounts and then cracking the hashes to retrieve plaintext passwords.  
- A 10-hour lifetime provides attackers with ample time to crack passwords, significantly increasing the success rate of the attack.  

While other settings (e.g., user ticket lifetime and clock tolerance) are standard or secure, the **service ticket lifetime must be reduced** to mitigate Kerberoasting risks.  

---

### **Attack Path: Kerberoasting**  
1. **Target Identification**: Attackers identify user accounts with valid service tickets (e.g., `host/server` or `domain\user`).  
2. **Service Ticket Request**: The attacker sends a request for a service ticket using a tool like `Get-ADUser` or `Impacket`.  
3. **Hash Extraction**: The attacker captures the service ticket’s hash (stored in the ticket).  
4. **Password Cracking**: The attacker uses the hash in a brute-force or rainbow-table attack to retrieve the plaintext password.  
5. **Leveraging Long Lifetime**: With a 10-hour service ticket lifetime, the attacker has more time to crack the password, increasing the attack’s success rate.  

---

### **Remediation**  
#### **Step 1: Reduce Service Ticket Lifetime**  
Set the **Maximum lifetime for service ticket** to a secure value (e.g., **1 hour or less**):  
1. Open **Group Policy Management Console (GPMC)**.  
2. Navigate to the GPO that manages Kerberos policies (typically a domain-wide or site-wide GPO).  
3. Go to:  
   - **Computer Configuration** > **Policies** > **Windows Settings** > **Security Settings** > **Account Policies** > **Kerberos Policy**.  
4. Edit the **"Maximum lifetime for service ticket"** setting:  
   - Change the value from **600 minutes** to **60 minutes** (1 hour) or less.  
5. Save the GPO and **force a policy update** on domain controllers (`gpupdate /force`).  

#### **Step 2: Verify & Monitor**  
- Check the GPO settings to confirm the change.  
- Monitor for Kerberoasting attempts (e.g., using SIEM tools or network traffic analysis).  

#### **Additional Recommendations**  
- **Restrict Service Accounts**: Limit the number of accounts with service tickets (e.g., avoid granting excessive permissions).  
- **Use Hash-Salted Passwords**: Enforce strong password policies to reduce the effectiveness of hash cracking.  

---

### **Why This Fix Matters**  
- **Kerberoasting** is a well-documented attack vector for Windows authentication systems.  
- A 10-hour service ticket lifetime is a **critical misconfiguration** that undermines security.  
- Reducing the service ticket lifetime to **≤1 hour** eliminates the large window for attackers to crack passwords, making Kerberoasting infeasible.  

By implementing this change, organizations significantly reduce their risk of Kerberoasting attacks while maintaining secure authentication workflows.
