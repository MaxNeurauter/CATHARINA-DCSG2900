### **3. Attack Path**
#### **Primary Risk: Kerberoasting & Silver Ticket Attacks**
- **Unconstrained Delegation (Kerberos-only)** allows the account to impersonate any service on behalf of any user in the domain.
  - An attacker who compromises this account (e.g., via phishing, brute force, or credential dumping) can:
    1. **Request a Service Ticket (TGS)** for any service in the domain (e.g., `ldap`, `cifs`, `http`).
    2. **Forge a Silver Ticket** (if the account has access to a service’s NTLM hash or via relay attacks).
    3. **Escalate privileges** if the delegated service runs under a high-privilege account (e.g., `SQL Server`, `Exchange`).
    4. **Lateral movement** by impersonating other users/services.

- **If the account is a Domain Admin or holds KRBTGT hashes**:
  - The attacker could generate **Golden Tickets** (full domain compromise).

#### **Technical Impact**:
- **Privilege Escalation**: Unauthorized access to sensitive services (e.g., AD, databases, email).
- **Lateral Movement**: Compromise of additional high-value accounts.
- **Persistence**: Long-term access via forged tickets.
