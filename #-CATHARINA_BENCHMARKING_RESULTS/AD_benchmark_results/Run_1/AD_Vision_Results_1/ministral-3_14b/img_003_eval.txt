### **3. Attack Paths & Technical Impact**
#### **A. AS-REP Roasting (CVE-2018-7730)**
- **Vulnerability:**
  - The `krbtgt` account **does not require Kerberos pre-authentication** (not shown as disabled in the screenshot).
  - Attackers can request **Service Principal Names (SPNs)** for users in unprotected groups and brute-force their passwords offline.
- **Impact:**
  - Full domain compromise via **Kerberos ticket forgery**.
  - Lateral movement to **Domain Admin** accounts.

#### **B. Privilege Escalation (Golden Ticket)**
- **Vulnerability:**
  - If an attacker obtains the **NTLM hash of `krbtgt`**, they can forge **Golden Tickets** with **Domain Admin privileges**.
  - Users like `arya.stark` and `brandon.stark` are in `Domain Admins` (high-value targets).
- **Impact:**
  - Persistent, undetectable domain dominance.

#### **C. Pass-the-Hash / Pass-the-Ticket**
- **Vulnerability:**
  - If `Allowed RODC Password Replication Group` is misconfigured, attackers can replicate credentials to **Read-Only Domain Controllers (RODCs)** and extract hashes.
- **Impact:**
  - Lateral movement across the domain.

#### **D. Brute Force on Default Accounts**
- **Vulnerability:**
  - `Administrator` and `Guest` accounts are enabled by default.
  - Weak or default passwords are common in such environments.
- **Impact:**
  - Initial access leading to **domain takeover**.

#### **E. Golden Certificate Attacks**
- **Vulnerability:**
  - Members of `Cert Publishers` can issue **malicious certificates**, bypassing authentication.
- **Impact:**
  - **Permanent persistence** (even after password changes).
