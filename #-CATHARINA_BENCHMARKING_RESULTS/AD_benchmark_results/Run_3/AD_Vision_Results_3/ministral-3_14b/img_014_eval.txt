### **3. Attack Path**
#### **Potential Attack Vectors:**
1. **Privilege Escalation via GPO Abuse**
   - If an attacker gains a **low-privileged account** (e.g., a standard user), they could **enumerate GPOs** and exploit misconfigurations.
   - If **Authenticated Users** have **Read** access to sensitive GPOs, an attacker could **discover misconfigured permissions** (e.g., overly permissive ACLs on other objects).
   - If **GPOs contain sensitive settings** (e.g., password policies, audit policies), an attacker could **extract and manipulate them**.

2. **Lateral Movement via GPO Persistence**
   - Attackers could **modify GPOs** (if they gain higher privileges) to **persist access** (e.g., adding malicious scripts via **Group Policy Preferences**).
   - If **Domain Admins or Enterprise Admins** have **custom permissions**, an attacker who compromises them could **escalate further**.

3. **Information Disclosure**
   - Since **Authenticated Users** can **read** the GPO, an attacker could **extract sensitive AD configurations**, aiding in **reconnaissance** before launching further attacks.

#### **Technical Impact:**
- **Low-privileged attackers** can **gather intelligence** on AD structure.
- **If combined with other misconfigurations** (e.g., **ACL abuse on AD objects**), this could lead to **full domain compromise**.
- **No direct critical vulnerability**, but **part of a broader attack chain**.
