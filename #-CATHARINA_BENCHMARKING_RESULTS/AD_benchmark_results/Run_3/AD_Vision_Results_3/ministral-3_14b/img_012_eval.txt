### **3. Attack Path**
#### **Potential Attack Vectors:**
1. **Privilege Escalation (Horizontal/Vertical)**
   - An attacker with a **low-privilege account** (e.g., a standard domain user) could exploit the **Authenticated Users** or **Everyone** permissions to modify ACLs, add themselves to the **Domain Admins** group, or escalate privileges.
   - **Technical Impact:**
     - Gain **Domain Admin** access.
     - Perform **Golden Ticket attacks** (Kerberos ticket forging).
     - **Lateral movement** across the domain.

2. **ACL Persistence & Backdoor Creation**
   - An attacker could **add themselves** (e.g., `lord.varys`) with **Full Control** and maintain persistence even if their initial access is revoked.
   - **Technical Impact:**
     - Long-term **domain compromise**.
     - Ability to **modify security settings** undetected.

3. **Exploitation of Pre-Windows 2000 Compatible Access**
   - This permission can be abused to **enumerate and manipulate group memberships** in older Windows systems or misconfigured environments.
   - **Technical Impact:**
     - **Group policy manipulation**.
     - **Unauthorized group additions**.

4. **Pass-the-Hash / Pass-the-Ticket Attacks**
   - If an attacker gains access to **any domain account’s hash**, they could use the **Authenticated Users** permission to **modify ACLs** and escalate privileges.
   - **Technical Impact:**
     - **Full domain takeover**.
     - **Data exfiltration or ransomware deployment**.
