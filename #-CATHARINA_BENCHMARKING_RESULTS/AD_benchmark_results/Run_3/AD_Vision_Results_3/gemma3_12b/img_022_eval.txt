**3. Attack Path**

The "Account is Sensitive" flag, when unchecked, indicates that the account does *not* have additional security restrictions applied. While it's not a direct vulnerability *itself*, its absence opens the door to several potential attack paths, significantly lowering the overall security posture. Here's the breakdown:

*   **Kerberoasting:** Accounts without the "Sensitive" flag are more susceptible to Kerberoasting. Kerberoasting allows an attacker to request Kerberos service tickets for accounts and use them to obtain the password hash of the account. This can be a starting point for lateral movement within the network.
*   **Pass-the-Hash:** If the Kerberoasting attack is successful, the attacker gains a password hash that can be used in a Pass-the-Hash attack to impersonate the user.
*   **Privilege Escalation:** If the account has elevated privileges, a successful Kerberoasting attack can lead to privilege escalation.

Here's a step-by-step guide to address this misconfiguration:
