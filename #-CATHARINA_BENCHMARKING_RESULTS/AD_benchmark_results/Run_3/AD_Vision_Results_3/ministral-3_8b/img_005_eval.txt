  A threshold of 5 invalid attempts is **too low** for most environments. Attackers can brute-force credentials more efficiently before lockout kicks in.

- **Short Lockout Duration (5 minutes):**
  A 5-minute lockout window allows attackers to **spam multiple accounts** before they are locked, increasing the likelihood of successful credential guessing.
  - *Best Practice:* **Minimum 30 minutes** (or longer in high-risk environments).

  The lockout counter resets too quickly, meaning an attacker can **retry the same account repeatedly** without facing prolonged consequences.
  - *Best Practice:* **Minimum 15-30 minutes** (or longer).
