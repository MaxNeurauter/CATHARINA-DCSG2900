### **3. Attack Path & Technical Impact**
#### **Primary Attack Vectors Enabled:**
##### **A. Kerberoasting (Mitigated but Possible with Long-Lived Tickets)**
   - **Impact**: Attackers can extract and crack hashed service tickets (SPNs) offline, leading to credential compromise.
   - **Why?** While Kerberoasting traditionally relies on **short-lived tickets**, long-lived service tickets (10 hours) increase the window for extraction before expiration.

##### **B. AS-REP Roasting (Likely Vulnerable if Pre-Authentication Required)**
   - **Impact**: If **User Account Control (UAC) → "Account is sensitive and cannot be delegated"** is **unchecked** (not shown here but often paired with Kerberos misconfigs), attackers can roast **AS-REP** hashes for password spraying.
   - **Why?** Long-lived user tickets (10 hours) + lack of **Pre-Authentication Required** (not shown) could enable this if other policies allow it.

##### **C. Forwardable/Delegated Ticket Abuse (Silver Ticket Risk)**
   - **Impact**: If **service tickets are forwardable** (not shown here but often tied to misconfigured constraints), attackers could forge **Silver Tickets** for privilege escalation.
   - **Why?** Long-lived service tickets (10 hours) + improper **constrained delegation** (not shown) create opportunities.

##### **D. Clock Skew Exploitation (Minor Risk)**
   - **Impact**: Attackers could exploit clock desynchronization (e.g., **7-minute tolerance**) to bypass time-based controls.
   - **Why?** While 7 minutes is reasonable, tighter tolerances (e.g., **1-3 minutes**) are recommended for high-security environments.
