### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**
❌ **Single Point of Failure (SPOF)**:
   - **Router1** is the sole Internet gateway—if it fails, the entire network loses Internet access.
   - **Switch0** is a critical bottleneck—if it fails, Switch1 and Switch2 segments lose connectivity to each other and the router.

❌ **No VLAN Segmentation**:
   - All devices (PCs, servers, laptops) are on the same broadcast domain, increasing **collision domains** and security risks (e.g., lateral movement by attackers).

❌ **No Redundant Links**:
   - No **Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) optimizations** (e.g., Rapid STP) or **link aggregation** between switches.
   - Linear topology creates **inefficient paths** if Switch0 fails.

❌ **Lack of Security Boundaries**:
   - No **firewall/ACL segmentation** between internal devices and the server.
   - No **DMZ or isolated guest network** for better security posture.

❌ **No High Availability (HA) or Failover**:
   - No redundant router or switch pairs (e.g., HSRP, VRRP, or switch clustering).

---

### **5. Recommendations (Industry Best Practices)**
#### **1. Implement VLAN Segmentation & Access Control Lists (ACLs)**
   - **Action**: Segment the network into **VLANs** (e.g., VLAN 10 for PCs, VLAN 20 for Servers, VLAN 30 for Guest/Internet-facing devices).
   - **Benefits**:
     - Reduces broadcast storms.
     - Isolates sensitive servers from general endpoints.
     - Enables **role-based access control (RBAC)** via ACLs.
   - **Implementation**:
     - Configure **Switch0, Switch1, Switch2** with VLANs and **802.1Q trunking** between switches.
     - Apply **firewall rules** (if a firewall is added) to restrict traffic between VLANs.

#### **2. Introduce Redundancy & High Availability (HA)**
   - **Action**:
     - **Add a redundant router** (e.g., Router2) with **HSRP/VRRP** for failover.
     - **Implement STP (Rapid PVST+)** to prevent loops and enable redundant switch links.
     - **Add a second uplink** from Switch1 to Switch0 (or Switch2) to create a **partial mesh**.
   - **Benefits**:
     - Eliminates SPOF at Router1 and Switch0.
     - Ensures **99.9%+ uptime** with automatic failover.
   - **Implementation**:
     - Configure **port aggregation (LACP)** between switches for bandwidth efficiency.
     - Set up **SSH/out-of-band management** for remote troubleshooting.

#### **3. Deploy a Firewall & DMZ for Security Hardening**
   - **Action**:
     - Insert a **firewall (e.g., Cisco ASA, pfSense, or Linux-based iptables)** between **Router1 and Switch0**.
     - Create a **DMZ VLAN** for the server (Server0) with **strict inbound/outbound rules**.
   - **Benefits**:
     - Protects internal devices from Internet-based attacks.

   - **Implementation**:
