### 4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)  
- **Single Points of Failure (SPOF)**:

- **Lack of Segmentation**:  
  - All endpoints share a single broadcast domain. Broadcast storms (e.g., from misconfigured devices) can degrade performance.  
  - No security boundaries exist: An attacker on *PC0* can directly access *PC4* without firewalls or VLANs.  
- **Inefficient Scaling**:  
  - The flat topology becomes a bottleneck as endpoints increase (e.g., 5+ PCs → 20+ PCs) due to shared switch resources.  
- **Security Vulnerabilities**:  
  - No firewall, IDS/IPS, or access control is visible. All devices are exposed to external threats via the router.  
- **Poor Resilience**:  
  - No redundancy (e.g., dual switches, router failover) is present. A single component failure causes full network downtime.
