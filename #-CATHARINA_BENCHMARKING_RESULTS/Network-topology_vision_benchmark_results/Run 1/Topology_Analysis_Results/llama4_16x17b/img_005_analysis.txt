- **Segmentation**: The network is divided into distinct zones (Internet, DMZ, LAN), which helps in applying different security policies based on the sensitivity of the resources.
- **Firewall Protection**: The presence of an ASA (Adaptive Security Appliance) between the Internet and the internal network provides a layer of protection against unauthorized access.

## 4: Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)
- **Single Point of Failure (SPOF)**: The design relies heavily on a single ASA for security and a single router for Internet connectivity. If either of these devices fails, it could lead to significant downtime.
- **Limited Redundancy**: There is no apparent redundancy in the design, which could improve availability. For example, having a backup Internet connection or a redundant path to critical resources could enhance resilience.
- **Potential for Broadcast Storms**: Without VLANs or other segmentation techniques within the LAN, there could be issues with broadcast traffic, although the use of switches mitigates this somewhat.

## 5: Recommendations
1. **Implement Redundancy**: Consider adding a redundant path for critical services, such as a secondary Internet connection or a backup ASA, to improve availability.
2. **VLAN Segmentation**: Implement VLANs within the LAN to segment traffic, reduce broadcast storms, and enhance security by limiting the scope of a potential breach.
3. **High Availability (HA) Pairs**: For critical devices like the ASA, consider implementing HA pairs to ensure minimal downtime in case of a failure.

By addressing these areas, the network can become more resilient, secure, and better equipped to handle growth and unexpected events.
