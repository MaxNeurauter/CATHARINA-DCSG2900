### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**

❌ **Flat Layer 2 Network (No VLANs)** – All devices are in the **same broadcast domain**, leading to:

   - **Broadcast storms** (inefficient traffic).
   - **No segmentation** between servers, PCs, and IoT (printers).
❌ **No DMZ or Security Zones** – Servers and endpoints share the same network, increasing exposure.
❌ **No High Availability (HA) for Critical Devices** – No backup router or switch clustering.

     - Improves traffic management.

#### **2. Introduce VLAN Segmentation & Access Control Lists (ACLs)**
   - **Create at least 4 VLANs**:

     - **VLAN 20**: Servers (with strict ACLs).
     - **VLAN 30**: Wireless (isolated from wired traffic).
     - **VLAN 40**: Printers (segmented for security).
   - **Implement Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**:
     - Restrict server-to-server, server-to-PC, and PC-to-Internet traffic.
     - Use **802.1X authentication** for wireless and wired ports.
   - **Benefits**:
     - Reduces attack surface.
     - Prevents broadcast storms.
     - Enables granular security policies.

#### **3. Add Redundancy & High Availability (HA)**

   - **Router HA**: Deploy **VRRP/HSRP** on the routers for automatic failover.
   - **Benefits**:
