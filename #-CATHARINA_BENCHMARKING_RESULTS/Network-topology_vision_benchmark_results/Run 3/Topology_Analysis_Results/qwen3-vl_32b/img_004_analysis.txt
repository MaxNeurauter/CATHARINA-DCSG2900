### 2. **Purpose & Architecture**  
The design is a **segmented corporate network with a DMZ**, intended to:  
- Isolate internal resources (LAN) from public-facing servers (DMZ).  
- Enforce security boundaries between the Internet, DMZ, and internal LAN via firewalls (ASAs).  
- Route traffic through a single router (2901) to the Internet.  
*No flat Layer 2 network or excessive redundancy is present.*  

---

### 3. **Strengths (Pros)**  
- **Strong Segmentation:** Clear logical separation between LAN, DMZ, and Internet via firewalls (ASAs).  
- **Security Boundaries:** ASAs act as critical control points to filter traffic between zones (e.g., DMZ-to-LAN, Internet-to-DMZ).  
- **DMZ Isolation:** Servers (Server0/Server1) are isolated from the internal LAN, reducing risk of external attacks spreading to critical assets.  
- **Logical Grouping:** Devices are grouped by function (e.g., all endpoints in LAN, servers in DMZ).  

---

### 4. **Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**  
- **Single Points of Failure (SPOFs):**  
  - **2901 Router0:** Failure halts *all* Internet traffic. No redundant router or HSRP.  
  - **2960-24TT Switch0 (DMZ):** Failure disconnects all DMZ servers.  
  - **2960-24TT Switch1 (LAN):** Failure isolates all LAN endpoints.  
- **Inefficient Firewall Architecture:**  
  - ASAs are in **series** (not a High Availability pair). If ASA0 fails, *all* traffic between Internet and DMZ/LAN stops (no failover).  
  - No load balancing or failover for ASA0/ASA1.  
- **Lack of VLAN Segmentation:**  
  - All LAN devices (PCs, IP phone) share a single switch (no VLANs). This risks:  
    - L2 broadcast storms.  
    - Unauthorized access (e.g., a compromised PC could attack the IP phone).  
- **No Redundancy in Critical Paths:**  
  - Single uplink between switches/routers (e.g., ASA0 → 2901 Router0).  
  - DMZ switch (2960-24TT Switch0) has no redundancy.  

---

### 5. **Recommendations**  
1. **Implement High Availability (HA) for Critical Devices:**  
   - Deploy **Active/Active or Active/Standby HA pairs** for ASAs and the 2901 Router (e.g., using HSRP or VRRP). This eliminates SPOFs and ensures uninterrupted traffic flow. *Example: Mirror ASA0/ASA1 as a pair, and add a 2901 backup router.*  
2. **Add VLAN Segmentation in the LAN:**  
   - Create VLANs on 2960-24TT Switch1 for:  
     - **VLAN 10:** PC traffic (PC0/PC1).  
     - **VLAN 20:** IP phone traffic (with QoS for VoIP).  
     - **VLAN 99:** Management (for switch/firewall access).  
   - This improves security, reduces broadcast domains, and optimizes traffic flow.  
3. **Enhance DMZ Redundancy:**  
   - Add a **second 2960-24TT switch** in the DMZ (e.g., stacked with Switch0) and connect both to ASAs. Use **LACP** for link aggregation to prevent single-switch failure from isolating servers.
