### 2. Purpose & Architecture  
The design is a **segmented corporate network with a DMZ** for public-facing services (e.g., server0). The firewall (ASA0) acts as a security boundary between the internal network (Switch2) and the rest of the infrastructure. The wireless network (AccessPoint-PT) and DMZ are logically separated from the internal LAN, supporting basic traffic segmentation.  

---

### 3. Strengths (Pros)  
- **Effective DMZ Segmentation:** Isolates public-facing resources (Server0, PC3) from the internal network, reducing attack surface.  
- **Hierarchical Structure:** Logical grouping (core, distribution, access) simplifies management and traffic flow.  
- **Basic Security Boundary:** ASA0 enforces security between internal and external segments.  
- **Wireless Integration:** Dedicated access point for mobile devices (Smartphone0).  

---

### 4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)  
- **Single Point of Failure (SPOF):**  
  - **ASA0 Firewall:** Only one device; failure cuts off all internal traffic to the core.  
  - **Switch0 & Switch1:** Each has a single uplink to the core (Switch0), creating a SPOF for the DMZ and internal segments.  
- **No Redundancy in Critical Path:**  
  - Switch2 (access layer) has no redundant uplinks to the core (only connects to Switch0).  
  - If Switch0 fails, **all traffic (internal, DMZ, and external) is disrupted**.  
- **Lack of VLANs:**  
  - All internal endpoints (PC0, PC1, PC2, Printer0) share the same broadcast domain on Switch2, increasing broadcast traffic and security risks (e.g., ARP poisoning, lateral movement).  
- **Inefficient DMZ Design:**  
  - The DMZ is connected via Switch1, which also handles internal traffic. If Switch1 fails, the DMZ is isolated from the firewall and core.  

---

### 5. Recommendations  
1. **Implement High Availability (HA) Pairs:**  
   - Deploy a **second ASA firewall** in active/standby mode (e.g., Cisco ASA HA) and **redundant uplinks** for Switch0/Switch1 (e.g., dual 1Gbps links to the core) to eliminate SPOFs.  
   - *Rationale:* Ensures continuity if a single device fails (per Cisco 3-Tier best practices).  

2. **Enforce VLAN Segmentation:**  
   - Create VLANs for:  
     - *Internal LAN* (PCs, printer, AP),  
     - *Wireless (WLAN)*,  
     - *DMZ (Server0, PC3)*,  
     - *Management (switch/router out-of-band access)*.  
   - Assign VLANs to Switch2/Switch1 and tag trunks to Switch0.  
   - *Rationale:* Reduces broadcast domains, improves security (e.g., isolates wireless traffic from critical servers), and aligns with hierarchical design principles.  

3. **Dedicated DMZ Connectivity:**  
   - Add a **second uplink from Switch1 to Switch0** (or a new core switch) and place the DMZ on a **dedicated firewall interface** (e.g., ASA0’s "DMZ" interface).  
   - *Rationale:* Ensures DMZ remains accessible during partial failures and separates public/internal traffic at the firewall (best practice for DMZs).
