### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**
❌ **Single Point of Failure (SPOF) at Switch1**:
   - If Switch1 fails, all downstream switches (Switch2, Switch3, Switch4) and their connected PCs lose connectivity.
   - No redundancy (e.g., no backup links or StackWise/StackPower for failover).

❌ **Flat Layer 2 Network (No VLAN Segmentation)**:
   - All devices (PCs, switches) appear to be in a **single broadcast domain**, increasing:
     - **Broadcast storms** risk.
     - **Security vulnerabilities** (lateral movement by attackers).
     - **Inefficient traffic handling** (all traffic floods to Switch1).

❌ **No Redundant Links**:
   - No loop-free alternate paths (e.g., no STP backup links between switches).
   - Router4 is a single uplink; if it fails, the entire subnet loses WAN access.

❌ **Lack of Security Boundaries**:
   - No firewall, ACLs, or VLAN separation between devices.
   - No DMZ or isolation for critical systems.

❌ **No High Availability (HA) or Link Aggregation**:
   - No EtherChannel/LAG between Switch1 and other switches or Router4.

❌ **Limited Scalability for Growth**:
   - Adding more devices may overwhelm Switch1’s capacity without redesign.

#### **2. Add Redundancy to Eliminate SPOF**

     - **Add a redundant uplink** from Switch2/Switch3/Switch4 to Router4 (or a second router).

   - **Benefits**:
     - Eliminates SPOF at Switch1.
     - Provides **failover paths** for upstream connectivity.

#### **3. Introduce Basic Security Measures**
   - **Action**:
     - **Deploy ACLs** on Router4 to restrict unnecessary traffic.
     - **Enable Port Security** on switches to prevent MAC flooding.
     - **Segment critical devices** (e.g., place a hypothetical server in VLAN 20 with strict access rules).
     - **Add a firewall** (e.g., Cisco ASA) between Router4 and the internet for stateful inspection.
   - **Benefits**:

#### **Bonus: Adopt a Hierarchical Design (Cisco 3-Tier Model)**
