#### **5. Weaknesses & Risks**  
- ❌ **Critical Single Points of Failure (SPOFs)**:  
  - **Switch3**: Central hub connecting all switches (4, 5, 6, 7). If fails, entire internal network is disrupted.  
  - **Switch6**: Only path to DMZ (ASAs + servers). Failure isolates DMZ from internal network.  
  - **Routers**: Router0 (Switch4) and Router1 (Switch5) have no redundant inter-switch path (e.g., if Switch4 fails, Router0 loses internal connectivity).  

- ❌ **Layer 2 Broadcast Storm Risk**:  
  - No VLAN segmentation. All devices (PCs, printers, APs, servers) share the same broadcast domain → **massive broadcast storms**, reduced performance, and potential security leaks.  
  - **Wireless Security Gap**: APs connected to Switch3 (same VLAN as wired PCs/printers) → Unsecured wireless traffic accessible to internal network.  

- ❌ **DMZ Redundancy Gaps**:  
  - ASAs (ASA0/ASA1) are connected to Switch6 but lack HA (High Availability) configuration. If one ASA fails, the DMZ may lose service (e.g., if no failover path exists).  

- ❌ **Inadequate Traffic Isolation**:  
  - Internal network lacks layer 3 segmentation (e.g., no ACLs between VLANs), risking unauthorized access to sensitive resources.  

---

#### **6. Recommendations**  
- **Eliminate SPOFs**:  
  - Replace Switch3 with a redundant distribution layer (e.g., Switch3 + Switch8 in active/active HA).  
  - Add a Layer 3 device (e.g., Cisco ISR) between Switch3 and Switch4/5 for traffic routing.  
- **Implement VLANs**:  
  - Segment traffic:  
    - VLAN 10: Wired workstations/printers (Switch7).  
    - VLAN 20: Wireless clients (APs connected to Switch3).  
    - VLAN 30: DMZ servers (Switch6).  
  - Enforce ACLs on ASAs to restrict access from internal to DMZ.  
- **Enhance DMZ Redundancy**:  
  - Configure ASA0/ASA1 in active/standby HA mode (e.g., failover during outages).  
- **Upgrade Routers**:  
  - Deploy a single ISR4331 with dual WAN interfaces for seamless failover between Router0 and Router1.  

---

### **Conclusion**  
The design has strong **security segmentation** (DMZ) and **redundancy** (dual routers) but suffers from **critical SPOFs** (Switch3/6) and **layer 2 scalability issues** (no VLANs). By implementing VLANs, redundant distribution switches, and HA configurations, the network can achieve enterprise-grade resilience and security. **Immediate priority**: address Switch3/6 SPOFs and add VLAN segmentation to prevent broadcast storms.
