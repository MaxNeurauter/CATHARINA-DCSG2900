### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**
❌ **Single Points of Failure (SPOF)**:
   - **Router0** is the only path to the internet; if it fails, the entire network loses connectivity.
   - **ASA2** is a single firewall; if it crashes, internal traffic cannot be filtered.
   - **No redundant switch links** (e.g., Switch0-Switch1, Switch1-Switch2) means a single switch failure isolates multiple devices.

❌ **Lack of Segmentation**:
   - All devices (PCs, Server0) appear on the same broadcast domain (no VLANs), increasing collision domains and security risks.
   - **Server0** is directly connected to Switch2 without isolation from workstations.

❌ **No Redundancy or High Availability (HA)**:
   - No failover mechanisms (e.g., HSRP, VRRP) for the router or firewall.
   - No loop prevention (e.g., STP) between switches (though not shown, it may be misconfigured).

❌ **Potential Security Gaps**:
   - **No DMZ** for public-facing services (if any exist).
   - **Flat network** increases attack surface (e.g., lateral movement by an attacker).

### **5. Recommendations (Industry Best Practices)**

   - **Benefit**:
     - Scalable and modular design.
     - Better traffic management and security enforcement.
