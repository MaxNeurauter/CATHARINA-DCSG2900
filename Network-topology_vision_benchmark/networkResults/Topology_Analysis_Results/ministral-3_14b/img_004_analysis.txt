### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**
❌ **Single Points of Failure (SPOF):**

❌ **Lack of Redundancy:**
   - No **failover mechanisms** (e.g., ASA HA pairs, HSRP/VRRP on routers, or redundant switches).
   - No **load balancing** for Internet connectivity or firewall throughput.

❌ **Potential Switching Loops:**
   - If **ASA0 and ASA1** are misconfigured (e.g., incorrect STP settings), loops could occur between the two firewalls and switches.

❌ **Limited Scalability:**
   - The **single 2960 switch** in the LAN and DMZ may not handle future growth efficiently.
   - No **VLAN segmentation** is visible, meaning broadcast domains are likely flat (increasing unnecessary traffic).

❌ **Weak VoIP Support:**
   - The **IP Phone0** is directly connected to the LAN switch without **QoS prioritization**, risking voice traffic degradation.

### **5. Recommendations**
#### **1. Implement Redundant Firewalls & High Availability (HA)**

#### **2. Introduce VLAN Segmentation & Layer 3 Switching**

   - Replace the **2960 switches** with **Layer 3 switches** (e.g., 3750/3850) to enable **inter-VLAN routing** and reduce firewall load.
   - Configure **STP (Rapid PVST+)** to prevent switching loops.

#### **3. Enhance QoS & Security for VoIP & DMZ Servers**

   - **Isolate DMZ servers further** by implementing **micro-segmentation** (e.g., separate VLANs for web, email, and database servers).
   - **Enable intrusion prevention (IPS)** on firewalls and **regular patch management** for servers.
