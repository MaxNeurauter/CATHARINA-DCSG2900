- The **Internet-facing router (2901)** connects to the perimeter firewall (ASA0(1)).

### **4. Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**
⚠ **Single Points of Failure (SPOF):**
   - **ASA0(1) and ASA0(3) are not in High Availability (HA) pairs** → If either fails, traffic drops.
   - **No redundant Internet link** → Single WAN router (2901) is a bottleneck.
   - **No redundant switches** → If a 2960 switch fails, its segment (LAN/DMZ) goes down.

⚠ **Lack of VLAN Segmentation:**
   - The **LAN and DMZ appear to be flat (single broadcast domains)** → No **micro-segmentation** (e.g., VLANs for VoIP, HR, Finance).
   - **IP Phone0(4) is on the same VLAN as PCs** → Potential for **VoIP traffic interference** and **security risks** (e.g., rogue devices).

⚠ **No Load Balancing:**
   - **Single ASA firewalls** → No **failover or load distribution** for high-throughput traffic.
   - **No redundant links** between ASA0(1) and ASA0(3) → If the link fails, DMZ-LAN communication stops.

⚠ **Potential Switching Loops:**
   - If **Spanning Tree Protocol (STP)** is not configured, loops could occur (though not visible in the diagram, it’s a risk in larger networks).

⚠ **No Explicit QoS Policy:**
   - **No QoS markings** for **VoIP (IP Phone0(4))** → Risk of **jitter, latency, and call quality issues**.

⚠ **DMZ Overlap Risk:**
   - The **Recruitment PC in DMZ** is unusual (typically, DMZ hosts **servers only**) → Could be a misconfiguration.

### **5. Recommendations (Industry Best Practices)**
#### **1. Implement High Availability (HA) for Critical Devices**
   - **Deploy ASA Firewalls in HA Pair (Active/Standby or Active/Active):**
     - Configure **ASA0(1) and ASA0(3) in Failover** to eliminate SPOF.
     - Use **stateful failover** to maintain active connections.
   - **Add a Redundant Internet Router:**
     - Introduce a **second WAN router (e.g., 2901 HA pair)** with **BGP or HSRP** for failover.

#### **2. Introduce VLAN Segmentation & Micro-Segmentation**
   - **Segment LAN into VLANs:**
     - **VLAN 10:** VoIP (IP Phones)
     - **VLAN 20:** General PCs
     - **VLAN 30:** Servers (if any in LAN)
   - **Implement VLAN Access Control Lists (VACLs) & Private VLANs:**
     - Restrict traffic between VLANs (e.g., PCs should not communicate with servers unless necessary).
   - **Use 802.1Q Trunking:**
     - Ensure **switch-to-switch and switch-to-firewall links are trunks** for VLAN separation.

#### **3. Enhance Security & Redundancy in DMZ & LAN**
   - **Move Recruitment PC out of DMZ (if not a server):**
     - DMZ should host **only servers** (e.g., web, email, VPN). Workstations belong in LAN.
   - **Deploy Redundant Switches with STP:**
     - Add a **second 2960 switch in LAN/DMZ** and configure **STP (RSTP/MSTP)** to prevent loops.
   - **Implement QoS for VoIP:**
     - **Mark VoIP traffic (CoS/DSCP)** and **prioritize it** on switches and firewalls.
   - **Enable Intrusion Prevention (IPS) on ASAs:**
     - Use **ASA IPS signatures** to detect and block attacks targeting DMZ/LAN.

#### **Bonus: Future-Proofing**
   - **Consider Software-Defined Networking (SDN) or Intent-Based Networking (IBN):**
     - Tools like **Cisco DNA Center** can automate segmentation and policy enforcement.
   - **Monitor with NetFlow/sFlow:**
     - Deploy **flow-based monitoring** to detect anomalies (e.g., DDoS, data exfiltration).
