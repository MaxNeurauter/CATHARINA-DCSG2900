### 4. **Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**  
- **Critical SPOF & Security Flaw**:  
  - **Server0 is exposed to the Internet** (bypasses the ASA2). Traffic to Server0 from the Internet **never passes through the firewall**, creating a **severe vulnerability** (e.g., if Server0 is a web server, it is directly accessible to attackers).

- **No Redundancy**:  
  - All critical devices (Router0, ASA2, Switch0, Switch1, Switch2) are **single instances**. A failure in any device (e.g., Switch0) disrupts traffic for all connected segments (e.g., Switch1 and its PCs lose access to Internet).  
- **Inefficient Segmentation**:  
  - The **lack of VLANs** means all devices on a switch (e.g., Switch2: PC2, PC3, Server0) share the same broadcast domain, increasing broadcast traffic and security risks.

- **Suboptimal Routing**: The 2901 Router0 is also handling the "core" role (e.g., routing between Switch0 and Switch2), which is **inefficient** for larger networks.
