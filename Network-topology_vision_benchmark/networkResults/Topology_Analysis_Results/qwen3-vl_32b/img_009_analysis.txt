### 4. **Weaknesses & Risks (Cons)**  
- **Single Points of Failure (SPOFs)**:

  - **Access Layer**: *All Access switches have only one uplink* to a single Distribution switch. A single cable/switch port failure causes segment outages.  
- **Critical Security Gaps**:  
  - **No Firewall or DMZ**: No security boundary between the Core and external networks (e.g., WAN), exposing the entire network to threats.  
  - **Lack of VLAN Segmentation**: No evidence of VLANs (e.g., red center in Distribution switches *may* indicate L3 functionality, but VLANs are not depicted). This risks broadcast storms and lateral movement of threats.  
- **Inefficient Design**:  
  - Overprovisioned Core (2 routers) but underprovisioned Distribution (4 single points of failure). The 3:1 ratio of Distribution-to-Access links is unbalanced.
